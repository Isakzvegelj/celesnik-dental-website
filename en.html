History & Legacy

Over 50 years of dedication to perfection

Our studio boasts the longest private dental tradition in Slovenia. It was founded by dr. Emil Čelesnik, who introduced the first advanced aesthetic porcelain techniques to our region and laid the foundation for modern aesthetic dentistry. Dr. Emil didn't just search for function — he sought absolute aesthetics, teeth that are not only healthy, but truly beautiful. His legendary masterpieces — such as the extraordinary reconstruction of a human skull for the Belgrade Congress in 1986 and the famous golden snake porcelain teeth presented in Nuremberg in 1989 — astounded the international professional community with an incredibly faithful imitation of natural teeth and supreme precision.

"Art is a faithful reproduction of natural reality."

Today, this rich legacy is continued with the same love, precision, and aesthetic sense by his daughter, dr. Nina Čelesnik. Her studio is equally dedicated to aesthetics and dental excellence — because your smile should not only be healthy, but truly enchanting. The studio is located in the heart of Bled, just steps away from the lake and all tourist attractions.
